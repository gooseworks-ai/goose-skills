AN ESSAY IN FIVE PARTS

Five Tips
for Building
a Startup
in 2026.

The old playbooks no longer apply. A new generation of founders is quietly rewriting what it means to build, ship, and scale in an age of abundant compute and scarce attention.

What separates the durable from the disposable? Five ideas, one issue.

01 · Distribution

Distribution
comes first,
always.

The product is no longer the moat. With AI compressing build cycles to days, attention is the scarcest resource — and it must be cultivated before a single line of code is written.

Build an audience first. A thousand true believers beats a polished launch to an empty room every single time.

02 · Compounding

Build in
public, but
with craft.

The transparent founder wins, but transparency without taste is noise. Share the work, the losses, the unvarnished decision trees — yet edit for the reader, not the feed.

Compounding audiences are built one honest sentence at a time. Quality of narrative outlasts quantity of posts.

03 · Leverage

Hire agents
before you
hire humans.

Every repeatable workflow is now a candidate for automation. Research, outreach, QA, ops — a solo founder with ten agents has the operating capacity of a Series A team from 2021.

The question is no longer “who do I hire?” — it is “what do I still do myself, and why?”

Read the essay →

04 · Focus

Pick the
smallest
possible wedge.

The temptation to build a platform is stronger than ever — resist it. A narrow, unignorable wedge beats a broad, forgettable suite in every market cycle.

Solve one problem so completely that a single customer would refuse to part with your product. The empire follows the beachhead.

05 · Longevity

Play the
long game,
stay weird.

The durable companies of this decade will not be the most hyped — they will be the ones still shipping in year seven, when the cycle has turned twice and the attention has moved on.

Optimize for a founder you still want to be in 2033. Taste, stamina, and a refusal to be boring are the only real moats left.
