Tip 01

Pick a Market,
Not a Problem

The best startups in 2026 choose markets that are structurally changing — AI disruption, regulation shifts, or demographic tailwinds — and then find the problem within that wave. Don't solve a pain in a shrinking pond.

Tip 02

Not Months

In 2026, your competitor has access to the same AI tools you do. Velocity is your moat. Build a ruthlessly small v1, get it in front of users within 30 days, and iterate based on real signal — not assumptions.

Tip 04

Distribute Before
You Build

The best distribution moat is built before the product launches. Grow an audience, a newsletter, or a community around the problem space — not your product. When you ship, you'll have a warm market waiting.

1

Write about the problem publicly for 90 days before launch

2

Build in public — share behind-the-scenes milestones weekly

3

Collect waitlist emails from every channel from day one

Tip 05

Charge Early,
Charge More

Free users give you feedback. Paying customers give you signal. Pricing reveals true willingness to pay, filters out low-intent users, and funds your next iteration. Start with paid from day one — discounts are easier to give than price increases to justify.

"Every startup I've seen fail from charging too much — I can count on one hand. From not charging enough — I've lost count."

— Patrick Collison, Stripe
