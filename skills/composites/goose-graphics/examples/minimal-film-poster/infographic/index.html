2026

Most startups fail in the first year. The ones that don't share a handful of quiet, counterintuitive habits. These are the five that matter most.

01

Customer Discovery

Talk to customers
before you build.

Spend your first thirty days in conversations, not in code. Most founders build solutions to problems that don't exist yet. The market will tell you what it needs — if you're willing to listen.

02

Product Strategy

Do fewer things
extraordinarily well.

A product that does one thing perfectly will always beat a product that does ten things adequately. Ruthless prioritization is not a limitation — it's your sharpest competitive advantage in 2026.

"Simplicity is the most sophisticated form of engineering."

03

Distribution

Distribution beats
product every time.

The best product doesn't win. The most distributed product wins. Build your go-to-market motion before your product is perfect. Channels compound. Features don't.

72%

of startups fail
from lack of demand

3×

growth for founders
who nail distribution first

04

Team & Culture

Hire people who
disagree with you.

Consensus is a startup killer. The best teams are intellectually combative, not comfortable. Hire for the capacity to challenge your assumptions — then actually listen when they do.

Intellectual honesty

Diverse perspectives

Speed of trust

05

Resilience

Default to doing.
Optimize later.

Paralysis by analysis is the silent killer of promising ideas. Ship something imperfect into the world. Reality will teach you more in a week of live usage than a month of planning ever could.

"The founders who survive are not the smartest. They are the ones who refuse to stop."
